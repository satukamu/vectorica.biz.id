Melatonin & Cellular Reset

Melatonin is widely known as the "sleep hormone," but its role in the human biological system goes far beyond inducing tiredness. It is the body's most powerful antioxidant, protecting mitochondria.

Mitochondrial Shielding

Within the cells, melatonin enters the mitochondria to neutralize reactive oxygen species (ROS) that accumulate during the day's metabolic activity. It is the primary "clean-up crew" of the cell.

Melatonin Optimization Pillars:

  • Morning Stimulus: Anchoring the anchor clock with sun.
  • Darkness Threshold: Eliminating blue light after sunset.
  • Temperature Drop: Lowering core temp to facilitate release.

Neural Scavenging

The protective effects of melatonin are particularly critical for the brain. Since the brain consumes vast amounts of energy, it produces significant metabolic waste that melatonin helps clear every night.

By mastering your melatonin architecture, you are unlocking the body's ultimate repair system. This leads to higher recovery states and ensuring you are ready for the challenges of the next day cycle.
